# Story Summary

Maya Chen runs Chen's Hearth, the neighborhood bakery her grandmother Lily built over thirty years in the Riverside District. When Derek Holt, CEO of Holt Foods, arrives offering to buy the building, Maya refuses — and discovers his "artisan" product line has been built on recipes stolen from small local bakeries, including a formula her grandmother quietly trademarked in 2001. As Derek escalates pressure through health citations, a smear piece, and a predatory licensing contract, Maya teams up with journalist Priya Nair, builds a documentation trail, and confronts Holt publicly at a city food expo. The settlement forces Holt to credit Lily's legacy and fund the very community he tried to displace.
